How it works

  1. Enter the loan amount, annual interest rate, and loan term (in years or months).
  2. Monthly payment = P × r(1+r)n / ((1+r)n − 1), where r = rate / 12 / 100 and n = total months.
  3. The amortization table breaks down each monthly payment into principal and interest components.

What is Loan Calculator?

A loan calculator computes monthly mortgage or loan payments based on the loan amount, interest rate, and loan term. It also generates a full amortization schedule showing how each payment splits between principal and interest over time.

How to use

  1. Step 1: Enter the loan amount (principal).
  2. Step 2: Input the annual interest rate as a percentage.
  3. Step 3: Set the loan term in months or years.
  4. Step 4: Click 'Calculate' to see the monthly payment and full schedule.

Use cases

  • Estimating monthly mortgage payments before buying a home.
  • Comparing different loan offers from multiple lenders.
  • Planning refinancing strategies and payoff timelines.
  • Understanding total interest paid over the life of a loan.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the interest rate monthly or annual?

The calculator uses an annual interest rate. It divides this by 12 months to calculate the monthly payment.

Does the schedule include property tax or insurance?

No, this calculator shows principal and interest only. For a full mortgage estimate, add estimated annual property tax and insurance to your annual costs.

How accurate is the result?

This uses the standard amortization formula. Actual payments may vary slightly due to rounding, PMI, or lender-specific terms.
