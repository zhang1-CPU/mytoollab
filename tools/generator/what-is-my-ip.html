Frequently Asked Questions

What is an IP address?

An IP address (Internet Protocol address) is a unique string of numbers that identifies your device on a network. It's like a postal address for your computer.

Why does the tool fetch my location?

Your public IP address can be used to approximate your geographic location. This tool uses an IP geolocation service to show your approximate city, region, and country.

Is my real IP address exposed?

Yes. This tool displays your public IP address as seen by external servers. If you want to hide it, you would need to use a VPN or proxy service.

What is IPv4 vs IPv6?

IPv4 is the older format (like 192.168.1.1) using 32 bits. IPv6 is the newer format (like 2001:0db8::) using 128 bits, allowing for many more unique addresses.

Does this show my exact location?

No. IP geolocation is only approximate, usually accurate to city or region level. It cannot pinpoint your exact street address.
