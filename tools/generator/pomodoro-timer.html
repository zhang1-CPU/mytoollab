Pomodoro Timer

A clean, keyboard-friendly Pomodoro timer. Customize work, short break, and long break durations — then stay in the zone with sound alerts and browser notifications.

Focus
25:00
Completed: 0 Long break every: 4 focus sessions

Settings

Keyboard shortcuts: Space to start/pause, R to reset, N to skip.

How it works

  1. Choose your focus, short break, and long break durations. Turn on sound alerts and browser notifications if you want.
  2. Click Start. A progress ring wraps around the countdown so you can see progress at a glance.
  3. After the configured number of focus sessions, a long break starts automatically. Otherwise, the timer alternates between work and short breaks.

What is the Pomodoro Technique?

The Pomodoro Technique breaks work into 25-minute focused sessions ('Pomodoros'), separated by 5-minute short breaks. After a set of sessions, take a longer (15–30 minute) break. It's designed to reduce mental fatigue and improve sustained attention.

How to use

  1. Step 1: Set your preferred durations (default 25 / 5 / 15 minutes).
  2. Step 2: Click 'Start' and focus until the timer beeps.
  3. Step 3: Take the break period and repeat.
  4. Step 4: Use keyboard shortcuts (Space, R, N) to keep your hands on the keyboard.

Use cases

  • Deep work sessions for writers, designers, and developers.
  • Managing ADHD and improving sustained attention.
  • Studying / exam prep with built-in rest periods.
  • Team-friendly sessions so everyone rests at the same time.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why 25 minutes?

25 minutes is long enough to get meaningful work done but short enough that you don't fatigue. You can adjust all durations in Settings.

Does it work when I switch tabs?

Yes — the timer continues in the background. If you enable browser notifications, you'll see an alert when a phase ends.

How do notifications work?

When you check 'Browser notifications', your browser asks for permission. Alerts appear through the standard OS notification center.
