About Text Analyzer

Our free text analyzer is a comprehensive writing tool that helps content creators, bloggers, students, and SEO professionals understand the characteristics of their text. Whether you are optimizing content for search engines, preparing academic papers, or simply wanting to improve your writing clarity, this tool provides detailed statistics and readability metrics that give you actionable insights into your text quality.

The analyzer calculates Flesch Reading Ease and Flesch-Kincaid Grade Level scores to determine how easy your text is to read. It also provides vocabulary diversity analysis, long sentence detection, and sentence length distribution charts that help you identify patterns in your writing style that might be hurting readability.

Key Features

  • Comprehensive statistics: Get word count, character count, sentence count, paragraph count, and average word and sentence lengths in one place.
  • Flesch Reading Ease score: Understand how easy your text is to read with a score from 0-100, along with grade level interpretation.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a good Flesch Reading Ease score?

For most general audiences, a score of 60-70 is ideal as it corresponds to plain English readable by 8th-9th graders. Technical content may score lower which is acceptable for specialist audiences.

How can I improve my readability score?

Use shorter sentences (under 20 words on average), prefer simpler words over complex ones, and break up long paragraphs. The long sentence detector helps identify specific sentences to revise.

What does vocabulary diversity mean for my writing?

Higher vocabulary diversity (50%+) suggests a varied vocabulary, while lower diversity indicates repetitive word usage. Aim for moderate diversity unless technical precision requires otherwise.

Can this tool help with SEO?

Yes. Search engines favor content that is easy to read. Using the readability analyzer to keep your content at a 7th-9th grade level can improve both user engagement and search rankings.

How accurate is the syllable counter?

The syllable counter uses an algorithm based on vowel groups and common English patterns. While highly accurate for standard English text, it may vary for technical terms, names, or non-English words.
