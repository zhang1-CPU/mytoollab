Lorem Ipsum Generator: Why Designers Use Placeholder Text

If you have ever looked at a design mockup or a website in development, you have seen "Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet..." filling spaces where real content will eventually go. But what exactly is lorem ipsum, where did it come from, and why do designers swear by it? This guide covers everything you need to know about dummy text and how to use a lorem ipsum generator effectively.

What Is Lorem Ipsum?

Lorem ipsum is a type of placeholder text used in graphic design, web design, publishing, and typesetting. It is a scrambled version of a Latin text that looks like real language but has no actual meaning. The purpose is to fill space in a layout so that designers can focus on visual design decisions without being distracted by the content.

The most familiar version starts like this:

Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet, consectetur adipiscing elit, sed do eiusmod tempor incididunt ut labore et dolore magna aliqua. Ut enim ad minim veniam, quis nostrud exercitation ullamco laboris nisi ut aliquip ex ea commodo consequat.

At first glance, it looks like Latin. But if you try to read it, you will quickly realize it is nonsense — words are cut off, sentences do not make sense, and there is no actual message. That is exactly the point. Your brain sees text, recognizes it as language, but does not try to read it — freeing you to focus on typography, spacing, and layout instead.

The Surprising History of Lorem Ipsum

Contrary to popular belief, lorem ipsum is not just random gibberish. It has roots in a real classical Latin text — Cicero's "De Finibus Bonorum et Malorum" (On the Extremes of Good and Evil), written in 45 BC.

The story goes that an unknown typesetter in the 1500s scrambled a passage from Cicero's work to create a type specimen book — a book showing off different typefaces and font sizes. Instead of using meaningful text that would distract from the type itself, he used scrambled Latin that looked like real text but had no meaning. That 500-year-old trick is still used by designers today.

The lorem ipsum we know today is derived from sections 1.10.32 and 1.10.33 of "De Finibus." The full original Latin passage has actual philosophical meaning — but the lorem ipsum version is mangled beyond recognition. It is a fascinating example of a design solution that has stood the test of time for over five centuries.

Fun fact. The word "lorem" is not a real Latin word — it comes from the word "dolorem" (pain, sorrow) that was split in the middle when the text was scrambled. The "ipsum" comes from "ipsum" (itself) in the original text. Together, "lorem ipsum" has no meaning in Latin at all.

Why Designers Use Placeholder Text

You might be wondering: why not just use real content? Or a simpler text like "Content goes here"? There are several good reasons designers prefer lorem ipsum:

It Lets You Focus on Design, Not Content

When you are designing a layout, the content is usually not ready yet. Using placeholder text lets you build the visual structure without waiting for the copy. You can iterate on font choices, line height, spacing, column widths, and overall composition — all without being influenced by what the text actually says.

It Prevents Content Bias

If you use meaningful text while designing, you naturally make design decisions based on the specific words you see. A clever headline might make you think a layout works better than it actually does. Lorem ipsum gives you a neutral baseline — you are designing for the shape and rhythm of text, not for specific content.

It Tests Typography Realistically

Good design is about how text looks at actual lengths. "Content goes here" is too short to test line breaks, paragraph flow, hyphenation, and readability. Lorem ipsum paragraphs give you realistic text length and word distribution so you can see how type actually behaves in your layout.

It Communicates "This Is a Draft"

Latin placeholder text is a universally understood signal that "the design is real but the content is not." Stakeholders immediately understand that they are looking at a prototype or mockup, and that the actual words will be filled in later.

It Fills Space Predictably

A good lorem ipsum generator lets you generate exactly the amount of text you need — a single word, a sentence, a paragraph, or 100 paragraphs. This predictability is essential for testing how layouts handle different amounts of content.

Alternatives to Standard Lorem Ipsum

While classic lorem ipsum is the most common placeholder text, it is not the only option. Depending on your project, you might prefer:

  • Business / startup ipsum: Uses business buzzwords and corporate jargon. Good for B2B website mockups where you want the placeholder to feel vaguely on-topic.
  • Custom placeholder: Some tools let you provide your own sample text, which the generator then remixes. Useful if you want text that matches your project's tone or vocabulary.
  • Specific language lorem ipsum: If you are designing for a non-English audience, you can find lorem ipsum variants in many languages that have the right word lengths and character distribution.
  • Image placeholders: For image-heavy designs, pair your dummy text with placeholder images to get a complete picture of the layout.

Best Practices for Using Placeholder Text

  1. Use realistic lengths. Do not just fill every space with the same amount of text. Think about how long the actual content will be and match that with your placeholder. A blog post should have several paragraphs; a button label should have a few words.
  2. Vary the length. Real content is not perfectly uniform. Use different paragraph lengths, include occasional long and short sentences, and test edge cases like very long words or very short content.
  3. Replace before launch. Lorem ipsum has one job — to be placeholder text. Never let it ship to production. Have a checklist item specifically for removing all placeholder content before launch.
  4. Use HTML tags when needed. If you are designing a web page, generate lorem ipsum that includes HTML formatting — headings, lists, bold, italic, links — so you can see how styled text looks in your layout.
  5. Do not overthink it. Placeholder text is a means to an end. The point is to help you design better, not to be perfect. Use a free lorem ipsum tool and move on to the actual design work.

When to Use Real Content Instead

Placeholder text is great, but there are times when real content is the better choice. Know when to switch:

  • When testing readability. Lorem ipsum does not tell you if your actual content is easy to read. Use real text for readability testing.
  • When presenting to stakeholders. Some clients or stakeholders find lorem ipsum distracting or unprofessional. Have at least one version with realistic sample content ready for presentations.
  • When content affects design. If the actual content has specific characteristics — very long titles, lots of numbers, technical terminology — you should test with real content early to make sure the design works for it.
  • When doing user testing. Real users interact with real content. For usability testing, always use content that is as close to final as possible.

What is lorem ipsum and why is it used?
