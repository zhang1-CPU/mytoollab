Frequently Asked Questions

What thumbnail resolutions does YouTube provide?

YouTube provides thumbnails in several resolutions: Max Res (1280×720 HD), SD (640×480), HQ (480×360), MQ (320×180), and Small (120×90). Not all videos have all resolutions available.

Can I download thumbnails from any YouTube video?

You can download thumbnails from any public or unlisted YouTube video. Private videos are not accessible since their thumbnails are restricted by the uploaders' privacy settings.

Are the downloaded thumbnails free to use commercially?

Thumbnails are sourced from YouTube's public servers but the copyright and rights to the thumbnail image belong to the video creator. You can use downloaded thumbnails for personal projects, but commercial use may require permission from the video creator.

Why doesn't a video have a max resolution thumbnail?

Some newer videos or videos with restricted embedding settings may not have the maximum resolution thumbnail available yet. YouTube processes thumbnails gradually, so waiting a few hours or days may make higher resolutions available.

What is the best thumbnail resolution for my project?

For video editing or design work where you need to overlay text, use the 1280×720 HD version. For quick previews or reference on mobile devices, smaller versions work fine and load faster.
