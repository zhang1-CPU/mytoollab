How it works

  1. Drop images into the zone, or click "Choose Images" to pick files. Multi-file uploads supported.
  2. Enter a target width or height — when "Lock aspect ratio" is on, the other dimension updates automatically based on the original image ratio.
  3. Choose an output format (JPG, PNG, WebP) and quality level. Quality only affects JPG and WebP.
  4. Click "Download" on individual images, or click "Download All" to bundle everything into a single ZIP.

What is Image Resizer?

Image Resizer is a browser-based tool for resizing and converting photos. It's handy for compressing images before uploading, adapting them for email attachments, or creating consistent-size thumbnails.

Use cases

  • Preparing social media and blog post images at specific dimensions.
  • Compressing photos before emailing or uploading to a CMS.
  • Converting PNG screenshots to JPG or WebP for smaller file sizes.
  • Resizing batches of product photos to consistent width/height.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are my images uploaded somewhere?

No. All image processing happens locally in your browser using the Canvas API. Files are never sent to a server.

What does the quality slider do?

It controls JPG and WebP compression quality (0.10 – 1.00). Lower values produce smaller files but with visible quality loss. PNG is always lossless.

What does "Preserve transparency" do?

When converting to PNG or WebP, transparent pixels are preserved. For JPG, transparency is replaced with white — the JPG format has no alpha channel.

How many images can I resize at once?

There's no hard limit. The tool is limited by the memory available in your browser — very large batches may work better in smaller groups.
