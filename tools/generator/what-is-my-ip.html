How it works

  1. Your browser queries a public IP service (ipify.org) to fetch your current IPv4 and IPv6 addresses.
  2. The remaining info is extracted directly from your browser using standard JavaScript APIs (no plugins, no server-side tracking).
  3. Nothing leaves your browser except the two HTTPS calls needed to fetch your public IPs.

What is What Is My IP?

A 'What Is My IP' tool reveals your public IP address, location, ISP, browser user agent, and connection details. It runs entirely in your browser and fetches public IP from an external API.

How to use

  1. Step 1: Open the page — your public IP is displayed automatically.
  2. Step 2: View your location (country, city), ISP, and connection type.
  3. Step 3: Check your browser's User Agent string.
  4. Step 4: Refresh to see updated information.

Use cases

  • Checking if you're using a VPN or proxy correctly.
  • Troubleshooting network connectivity issues.
  • Verifying your apparent geographic location for geo-restricted services.
  • Testing whether your ISP assigns static or dynamic IPs.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between public and private IP?

A public IP is assigned by your ISP and is visible on the internet. A private IP (e.g., 192.168.x.x) is assigned by your router and only works within your local network.

Does this tool log my IP address?

No personal data is stored. The tool only reads your IP for display purposes.

Why is the shown location inaccurate?

IP geolocation is approximate and can be off by tens to hundreds of kilometers. It identifies your ISP's region, not your exact address.
