Frequently Asked Questions

How does text comparison work?

The tool uses the Longest Common Subsequence (LCS) algorithm to find matching and differing characters between two texts. It calculates similarity as a percentage.

What does similarity percentage mean?

Similarity percentage shows how much of the two texts match. 100% means identical texts, 0% means completely different. 80%+ suggests high similarity.

Can I detect plagiarism with this?

This tool highlights differences between two texts, which can help identify copied content. However, it cannot search databases for original sources.

What do the colors mean?

Green shows matching text. Yellow indicates changed portions. Red shows text that was removed or doesn't match.

Is my text stored?

No. All comparison happens locally in your browser. Your texts are never transmitted or stored anywhere.
