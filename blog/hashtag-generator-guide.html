Hashtag Generator: How to Get More Followers With the Right Tags

Hashtags are one of the most powerful — and most misunderstood — tools for growing your social media audience. Use the right hashtags and your content can reach thousands of new people who are genuinely interested in what you post. Use the wrong ones and you get lost in the noise. In this guide, we break down how to find good hashtags, build a winning hashtag strategy, and use a hashtag generator to grow your following organically on Instagram, TikTok, and beyond.

Why Hashtags Still Matter in 2026

You might have heard people say hashtags are dead. They are not. Hashtags remain one of the best ways to get your content in front of people who do not already follow you. They are how the algorithm understands what your content is about and who might want to see it.

That said, hashtag strategy has changed a lot over the years. The old approach — spamming 30 of the most popular hashtags you can find — does not work anymore. In fact, it can hurt your reach. Modern hashtag strategy is about relevance, specificity, and mixing different sizes of tags.

Whether you are growing a personal brand, promoting a business, or just want more people to see your creative work, a good hashtag strategy can put your content in front of the right audience. And a good hashtag generator can make the process much faster.

The 3 Types of Hashtags You Need

A good hashtag mix includes three categories: small, medium, and large. Each serves a different purpose, and combining them gives you the best chance of getting seen.

Small/Niche Hashtags (under 100K posts)

These are highly specific, targeted hashtags with smaller communities. Think #SeattleCoffeeShops instead of #Coffee. The audience is smaller but much more engaged and relevant.

Because fewer people use these hashtags, your content is less likely to get lost in the noise. You have a much better chance of appearing in the "Top" section or even being seen by people who browse that hashtag regularly.

Medium Hashtags (100K to 1M posts)

Medium-sized hashtags give you a balance of reach and competition. They have enough posts that lots of people are looking at them, but not so many that your content disappears instantly.

If your content performs well in smaller hashtags, it might start appearing in medium ones too, giving you a boost in reach.

Large/Broad Hashtags (1M+ posts)

These are the big, popular hashtags with millions of posts. Your chances of getting noticed in just the hashtag feed are slim, but they help the algorithm understand what your content is about and categorize it properly.

Use 1-3 broad hashtags for context, but do not rely on them for reach. The real growth happens in the smaller and medium tags.

The ideal mix: Aim for roughly 50% small/niche, 30% medium, and 20% large/broad hashtags. This gives you the best of both worlds — targeted reach from small tags and algorithm context from large ones.

How to Find Good Hashtags for Your Niche

The best hashtags are the ones your target audience is actually following and browsing. Here is how to find them:

1. Start with Your Competitors and Peers

Look at accounts similar to yours — same niche, similar size, same target audience. What hashtags are they using? Make a list of the ones that appear repeatedly. These are probably working for them, and they might work for you too.

Do not just copy their entire hashtag list — use it as a starting point for your own research.

2. Use Instagram's (or TikTok's) Suggestions

When you type a hashtag into the search bar, the platform suggests related hashtags. This is a goldmine for finding relevant tags you might not have thought of. Click through and check the post count and the type of content that appears in each one.

3. Check Hashtag Pages for Related Tags

On Instagram, when you visit a hashtag page, there is a "Related" section at the top with similar hashtags. Click through these and you can discover entire networks of niche hashtags relevant to your content.

Building Your Hashtag Strategy for Growth

Good hashtags are not enough — you need a strategy. Here is how to build one that actually grows your following.

Step 1: Define Your Niche and Audience

Before you can find the right hashtags, you need to know exactly who you are trying to reach. What are their interests? What problems do they have? What other accounts do they follow?

The more specific you can be, the better. "People who like fitness" is too broad. "Moms in their 30s who do home workouts with minimal equipment" is a niche you can target effectively.

Step 2: Build Multiple Hashtag Sets

Do not use the same 30 hashtags on every post. Create 3-5 different sets tailored to different types of content you post.

For example, a food blogger might have:

  • Recipe posts set
  • Restaurant review set
  • Kitchen gadget set
  • Healthy eating set

Each set has 15-25 carefully chosen hashtags relevant to that specific type of content.

Step 3: Track What Works

The biggest mistake people make with hashtags is not measuring results. You need to track which hashtag sets perform best and double down on what works.

For each post, note which hashtag set you used and track:

  • Reach from hashtags (check Instagram Insights)
  • Number of new followers gained
  • Engagement rate
  • Saves and shares

Over time, you will see patterns. Some hashtags will consistently drive more reach and followers. Keep those. Drop the ones that do not perform.

Step 4: Refresh Regularly

Hashtag popularity changes over time. New ones emerge, old ones get oversaturated, and algorithm preferences shift. Refresh your hashtag sets every 2-3 months to keep up.

Add new hashtags you discover, drop ones that are not performing, and keep testing. Hashtag strategy is not a set-it-and-forget-it thing — it is an ongoing process.

Quality over quantity: It is better to have 10 highly relevant hashtags than 30 vaguely related ones. The algorithm rewards engagement, and you get more engagement from a smaller, more targeted audience than a huge, random one.

Hashtag Mistakes That Hurt Your Growth

Bad hashtag strategy can be worse than no strategy at all. Avoid these common mistakes:

  • Using only huge hashtags. #love, #instagood, #photooftheday — these have hundreds of millions of posts. Your content will be gone in seconds. Use them sparingly for context, not for reach.
  • Using the exact same set on every post. The algorithm might flag this as spammy behavior. Mix it up with different sets for different content types.
  • Ignoring niche communities. The real growth happens in small, active hashtag communities. Find the ones in your niche and become a regular contributor.
  • Hashtag stuffing in captions. Putting 30 hashtags in your caption looks spammy and is hard to read. Put them in the first comment instead, or keep them at the end of the caption separated by periods.
  • Using banned or restricted hashtags. Some hashtags are banned or have restricted visibility. If you use them, your post might not show up at all. Always check a hashtag before using it.
  • Not matching hashtags to content. Your hashtags should accurately describe your content. Using popular but unrelated tags to get views will hurt your engagement rate and tell the algorithm you are not relevant.

Hashtags by Platform: Instagram vs. TikTok vs. Others

Hashtags work differently on different platforms. Here is what you need to know:

Instagram

Instagram is still the hashtag king. You can use up to 30 hashtags per post, but most accounts do best with 10-20 well-chosen ones. Instagram hashtags appear on the post itself, in Stories, in Reels, and in your bio (as clickable links).

On Instagram, small and medium hashtags are where you get the most value. The algorithm also uses hashtags to categorize your content and show it to people who do not follow you via the Explore page.

TikTok

TikTok hashtags work similarly but tend to be more about trends and challenges. TikTok hashtags generator tools often focus on trending sounds and challenges as much as niche topics.

On TikTok, 3-5 hashtags per video is usually enough. Mix 1-2 big trending hashtags with 2-3 niche ones. The TikTok algorithm is heavily weighted toward watch time and completion rate, so hashtags are less important than content quality — but they still help you get discovered initially.

Twitter/X

Hashtags on Twitter work best for joining conversations and events. 1-2 hashtags per tweet is usually optimal. More than that can hurt engagement. Use them to tap into trending topics and relevant conversations.

LinkedIn

LinkedIn hashtags are great for professional content and B2B marketing. 3-5 relevant hashtags per post is a good number. LinkedIn users actively follow hashtags in their industry, so good tags can put your content in front of decision-makers.

Beyond Hashtags: Real Organic Growth

Hashtags are a powerful tool, but they are not magic. The foundation of social media growth is still great content that people want to engage with. Hashtags get your content in front of people — it is up to your content to turn those viewers into followers.

Here is the real growth formula:

  • Great content + right hashtags = discovery. People see your post through hashtags.
  • Discovery + engagement = followers. They like what they see and follow you.
  • Followers + consistency = growth. You keep posting great content, your followers engage, the algorithm shows you to more people.

Hashtags are just the first step. But they are an important first step — especially for new accounts that do not have an existing audience yet.
