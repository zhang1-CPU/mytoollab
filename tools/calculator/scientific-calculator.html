What is Scientific Calculator?

A scientific calculator performs advanced mathematical operations including trigonometry (sin, cos, tan), logarithms, exponents, roots, factorials, and more. It supports both degrees and radians for trigonometric calculations.

How to use

  1. Step 1: Use the numeric pad for basic input.
  2. Step 2: Select DEG or RAD mode for trigonometric functions.
  3. Step 3: Click function buttons (sin, cos, log, etc.) for advanced operations.
  4. Step 4: Use parentheses for complex expressions.
  5. Step 5: Press '=' to evaluate.

Use cases

  • Engineering and physics homework and coursework.
  • Scientific research calculations and data analysis.
  • Financial modeling with exponential functions.
  • Programming and computer science calculations.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between DEG and RAD?

DEG (Degrees) uses 0-360° for angles. RAD (Radians) uses 0-2π. Use DEG for everyday use; RAD for advanced math and calculus.

Can I use constants like π and e?

Yes. Click the π button for Pi (3.14159...) and the e button for Euler's number (2.71828...).

Does it support parentheses?

Yes, you can nest multiple levels of parentheses for complex expressions.
