What is Pomodoro Timer?

The Pomodoro Technique is a time management method that breaks work into 25-minute focused sessions ('Pomodoros') followed by 5-minute breaks. A Pomodoro timer tracks these intervals and signals when to work and when to rest.

How to use

  1. Step 1: Click 'Start' to begin a 25-minute work session.
  2. Step 2: The timer counts down automatically.
  3. Step 3: When the session ends, a notification sounds and the break timer starts.
  4. Step 4: Click 'Skip' to move to the next phase, or 'Reset' to start over.

Use cases

  • Beating procrastination and maintaining focus on tasks.
  • Managing ADHD and improving concentration.
  • Breaking large projects into manageable time blocks.
  • Preventing burnout by enforcing regular breaks.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I customize the timer lengths?

Yes. Most Pomodoro timers allow you to adjust the work session (default 25 min), short break (5 min), and long break (15 min) durations.

Does the timer work if I switch tabs?

Yes. The timer runs in the background using JavaScript intervals. However, for best results, keep the tab open.

What is a long break?

After every 4 Pomodoro sessions, take a longer break of 15-30 minutes to rest and recharge.
