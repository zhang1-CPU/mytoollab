Why Compress PDFs?

  • Email attachments: Most email services limit attachments to 10-25MB. Compression keeps your documents within limits.
  • Faster uploads: Smaller PDFs upload faster to cloud storage, LMS platforms, and web forms.
  • Save storage space: Reduce disk usage on your computer, phone, and cloud storage.
  • Better mobile experience: Compact PDFs load faster on mobile devices with limited data plans.
  • Privacy first: All processing happens in your browser. Nothing is uploaded to external servers.

Frequently Asked Questions

How to compress a PDF file?

Upload your PDF, choose a compression level, and download the smaller PDF instantly. Everything works in your browser.

Is my uploaded PDF stored?

No. All compression happens locally in your browser. Your PDF is never uploaded to any server.

What is the maximum file size?

Files up to 50MB work well in-browser. For larger files, reduce image quality in advanced settings.

Can I compress multiple PDFs?

Yes. Upload multiple PDFs at once. Each file is compressed separately and you can download them individually.

Will compression reduce PDF quality?

Low compression preserves almost all quality. Medium and High compression reduce image resolution which is usually unnoticeable on screen but may affect professional printing.
