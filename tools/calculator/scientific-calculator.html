What is Scientific Calculator?

A scientific calculator performs advanced mathematical operations including trigonometry (sin, cos, tan, and their inverses), logarithms (base-10 and natural), exponents, roots, factorials, and more. The angle mode (Degrees or Radians) controls trig input/output.

How to use

  1. Use the numeric pad for basic input; you can also type directly on your keyboard (0-9 + - * / . ( ) ^ ! Enter Backspace Esc).
  2. Select DEG or RAD mode depending on whether your trigonometric input is in degrees or radians.
  3. Click function buttons (sin, cos, log, ln, sqrt, etc.) — they automatically append a parenthesis. Close parentheses manually.
  4. Use xʸ for exponentiation and n! for factorials. Use π and e for constants.
  5. Press = or Enter to evaluate. The result appears at the bottom; the original expression appears at the top.

Use cases

  • Engineering and physics homework and coursework.
  • Scientific research calculations and data analysis.
  • Financial modeling with exponential functions and logarithms.
  • Programming and computer science calculations.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between DEG and RAD?

DEG (Degrees) interprets trig input using 0-360° per full circle. RAD (Radians) uses 0-2π. Use DEG for everyday unit conversions; use RAD for calculus and advanced math.

Can I use constants like π and e?

Yes. Click π for Pi (3.14159…) and e for Euler's number (2.71828…). The eˣ button inserts exp(, which computes e raised to a value.

Does it support parentheses?

Yes. You can nest multiple levels of parentheses for complex expressions.

What is the operator precedence?

Parentheses first, then functions and factorials, then exponentiation right-associative, then × ÷, then + −. Unary minus is supported.
