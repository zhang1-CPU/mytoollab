Frequently Asked Questions

What is Flesch Reading Ease?

Flesch Reading Ease scores range from 0-100. Higher scores mean easier reading. Score of 60-70 is 'Standard' (8th-9th grade), while 90+ is 'Very Easy' (5th grade level).

What is Flesch-Kincaid Grade Level?

This estimates the U.S. school grade level needed to understand the text. For example, a score of 8 means an 8th grader should be able to read it.

How is reading time calculated?

Reading time is estimated using words divided by reading speed. Slow readers: 200 WPM, Average: 250 WPM, Fast: 300 WPM.

What counts as a long sentence?

Sentences over 30 words are marked as very long (red), and sentences over 20 words are marked as long (orange). Shorter sentences generally improve readability.

What does vocabulary diversity mean?

It's the ratio of unique words to total words. Higher diversity (50%+) suggests a varied vocabulary, while lower diversity suggests repetitive word usage.
