FAQ

How do you calculate X percent of a number?

Multiply the number by the percentage and divide by 100. Example: 25% of 200 = (25 × 200) ÷ 100 = 50.

How do I find what percentage X is of Y?

Divide X by Y, then multiply the result by 100. Example: 50 out of 200 = (50 ÷ 200) × 100 = 25%.

How do you calculate percentage change?

Subtract the original number from the new number, divide by the original number, and multiply by 100. A positive result means an increase; a negative result means a decrease.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate a discount?

Multiply the original price by the discount percentage, then divide by 100. Subtract the result from the original price for the final sale price.

What's the formula for percentage increase?

Percentage increase = ((New Value - Original Value) / Original Value) × 100.

Can I reverse-calculate the original number?

Yes. If you know the result and the percentage, divide the result by the percentage and multiply by 100.
