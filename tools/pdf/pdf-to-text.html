PDF to Text

Upload a PDF and extract its text content locally in your browser. Supports text objects, standard fonts, and compressed streams. Nothing is sent to a server.

No file selected. Note: only text embedded as actual PDF text objects can be extracted — scanned documents with only image content cannot be converted without OCR.

How it works

  1. Select a PDF from your device.
  2. Page and text objects are parsed locally in your browser using pure JavaScript.
  3. The extracted text appears in the output area; copy it or download as a .txt file.

What this tool extracts

This tool reads PDF text-showing operators (Tj, TJ and related) and returns them as plain text. It handles indirect objects, streams with standard Flate compression, simple font encodings, and common ToUnicode / WinAnsi / StandardEncoding mappings. Complex layout, ligature substitution, CJK fonts using embedded CID maps, and image-only (scanned) documents may not extract cleanly — for those, an OCR tool is needed.

How to use

  1. Step 1: Click 'PDF File' and choose a .pdf file from your device.
  2. Step 2: Wait for the document to be parsed locally.
  3. Step 3: Review the extracted text in the output area.
  4. Step 4: Copy to clipboard or save as a .txt file.

Use cases

  • Extracting article text from PDFs for quotation or summarization.
  • Searching through PDF content without needing a PDF reader.
  • Converting PDF reports into plain text for further processing.
  • Pulling citations and references from PDFs.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is my PDF uploaded to a server?

No. The entire PDF is parsed inside your browser. It is never sent to any server.

Why is some text missing or garbled?

PDFs without a reliable encoding map (no /ToUnicode / /Encoding) or with specialized CJK/CID fonts cannot always be converted to readable text. Scanned documents contain only pixel data — they require OCR to extract text.

Does it preserve page breaks?

Yes. Text from different pages is separated by a page marker ("--- Page N ---") so you can retain structural boundaries in the output.

About PDF to Text Converter

Our free PDF to text converter extracts the textual content from PDF documents and presents it as clean, readable text that you can copy, search, or process further. Unlike screen capture tools that capture images, this converter actually reads the text layer of the PDF, making the output fully editable and searchable — no retyping required.

The tool processes PDFs entirely in your browser without uploading them to any server, ensuring complete privacy for sensitive documents like legal contracts, medical records, or business correspondence. Whether you need to extract text for translation, data entry, content repurposing, or simply to make PDF content accessible in a more portable format, this utility delivers results instantly.

Key Features

  • Accurate text extraction: Reads and extracts the actual text layer from PDF documents, preserving the original content without images or formatting artifacts.
  • Complete document extraction: Extracts text from all pages in the PDF, concatenating content in the correct reading order.
  • Fully local processing: All PDF parsing happens in your browser — your documents never leave your device.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will this tool extract text from scanned PDFs?

Standard text extraction works only on PDFs with an actual text layer. Scanned PDFs that are essentially image files will not have extractable text — for those, you would need OCR (Optical Character Recognition) software.

Does the extracted text preserve formatting like bold or italics?

No. Plain text extraction removes all formatting. If you need to preserve formatting, consider using our PDF to Image converter to capture a visual representation instead.

Can I extract text from specific pages only?

Currently the tool extracts text from all pages in the PDF. For selective page extraction, you could use our Split PDF tool to extract specific pages first.

What about text encoded with non-standard fonts?

Some PDFs use custom or non-standard fonts that may not render correctly during text extraction. In such cases, the extracted text may show replacement characters or garbled output.

Is my PDF stored or logged anywhere?

No. All processing occurs locally in your browser's memory. Your PDF file is never uploaded to any server and is not stored after you close the page.
