How it works

  1. Choose the unit system (Metric or Imperial) and enter your weight and height.
  2. BMI = weight(kg) / height(m)² — or 703 × weight(lb) / height(in)² for imperial.
  3. Results update live as you type; your category is color-coded for quick reference.

Frequently Asked Questions

What do BMI categories mean?

BMI categories are: Underweight (below 18.5), Normal weight (18.5-25), Overweight (25-30), and Obese (above 30). These are WHO standard reference ranges.

Is BMI accurate for athletes?

BMI may not be accurate for athletes or muscular people, as it doesn't distinguish between muscle and fat mass. It also doesn't account for age, gender, or ethnicity.

Can I use imperial units?

Yes. Toggle between Metric (kg/cm) and Imperial (lb/in) using the tabs at the top of the calculator.

Is my data saved?

No. All calculations are performed locally in your browser. Your weight and height are never transmitted or stored.

About BMI Calculator

Our free BMI calculator provides a quick and reliable way to assess your body weight relative to your height using the widely-recognized Body Mass Index formula. BMI is a commonly used screening tool by healthcare professionals to categorize individuals as underweight, normal weight, overweight, or obese. Simply enter your height and weight, choose between metric or imperial units, and receive your BMI value along with the corresponding category within seconds.

The calculator supports both metric (kilograms and centimeters) and imperial (pounds and inches) measurement systems, making it accessible regardless of which country you are in. The results are based on World Health Organization BMI classification standards, which provide consistent categories for adults aged 18 and older. While BMI is not a direct measure of body fat, it serves as a useful initial indicator for discussing health risks associated with weight.
