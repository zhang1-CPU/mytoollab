Frequently Asked Questions

What is keyword density?

Keyword density is the percentage of times a keyword or phrase appears compared to the total number of words in a piece of content. It's used to determine if content is over-optimized for SEO.

What is the ideal keyword density?

The generally recommended keyword density is 1-3%. Above 5% may be considered keyword stuffing by search engines and could negatively affect your SEO.

What are stop words?

Stop words are common words like 'the', 'a', 'is', 'to' that don't add semantic value. Filtering them helps focus on meaningful keywords.

How is keyword density calculated?

Density = (Keyword Count / Total Words) × 100. For example, if 'SEO' appears 5 times in 200 words, the density is 2.5%.

Can I analyze multiple keywords?

Yes. The tool shows all unique words sorted by density. You can see the top keywords and their percentages at a glance.

About Keyword Density Checker

Our free keyword density checker is an essential SEO tool that helps content writers, marketers, and website owners analyze how often specific words and phrases appear in their content. By calculating the percentage of times a keyword appears relative to the total word count, you can ensure your content is properly optimized without crossing into keyword stuffing territory that search engines penalize.

The tool automatically filters common stop words to focus on meaningful keywords, displays all unique words sorted by frequency, and highlights keywords that may be over- or under-optimized. Whether you are writing blog posts, optimizing product descriptions, or auditing existing content, this analyzer provides the insights you need to balance readability with SEO effectiveness.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the ideal keyword density for SEO?

The generally recommended keyword density is 1-3% for your primary keyword. Above 5% may be considered keyword stuffing by search engines and could negatively affect your rankings. The ideal varies by content length and competition.

How is keyword density calculated?

Density = (Keyword Count / Total Words) × 100. For example, if your primary keyword appears 5 times in 200 words, the density is 2.5%.

What are stop words and why filter them?

Stop words are common words like "the", "a", "is", and "to" that search engines typically ignore. Filtering them helps you focus on meaningful keywords and get a more accurate picture of your content's topical relevance.

Can I analyze multiple keywords at once?

Yes. The tool displays all unique words sorted by frequency, so you can see the density of any keyword or phrase in your content at a glance.

Does keyword density still matter for modern SEO?

Keyword density is just one of many SEO factors. While search engines have become more sophisticated and semantic relevance matters more than exact keyword matching, proper keyword usage still signals topical relevance to search algorithms.
