Frequently Asked Questions

What image formats are supported?

This tool supports JPG (JPEG), PNG, WebP, and GIF images. Choose the best format based on your needs: JPG for photos, PNG for graphics with transparency, WebP for best compression.

Does compression reduce image quality?

Higher compression means smaller file size but may reduce quality. The preview shows the result before downloading so you can adjust quality settings to balance size and quality.

How much can I reduce my image size?

Reduction depends on the original image and chosen quality setting. JPGs with high quality settings may compress 50-80%. PNGs are lossless but may still compress somewhat depending on content.

Are my images uploaded to a server?

No. All compression happens locally in your browser. Your images never leave your device.

Can I compress multiple images at once?

This tool processes one image at a time. For batch compression, you would need to use our tool multiple times or use a desktop application.

How do I compress images without losing quality?

Use a higher quality setting (80-90%) for minimal quality loss that is barely noticeable to the human eye. Our tool provides a side-by-side preview so you can compare the original and compressed versions before downloading.

Which image format is best for web?

WebP format offers the best compression-to-quality ratio for web use. For photos, use WebP or JPG. For images with transparency or sharp edges, use PNG. We recommend 70-85% quality for optimal web performance.

Can I compress photos from my phone?

Yes, our image compressor works perfectly on mobile devices. Simply visit the page in your mobile browser, select a photo from your gallery, and download the compressed version — no app installation needed.

Is there a limit to how many images I can compress?

No, there is no limit. You can compress as many images as you need, completely free. Each image can be up to 20MB in size for optimal performance.

How long does image compression take?

Compression speed depends on image size and your device. Most images compress within a few seconds. Larger, high-resolution photos may take slightly longer but typically complete in under 30 seconds.

How do I optimize images for my website?

For best web optimization, resize images to their actual display dimensions, use WebP format with 70-85% quality. This dramatically reduces file sizes while maintaining excellent visual quality, improving page load times and SEO rankings.
