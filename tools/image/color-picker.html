How it works

  1. Upload an image (JPG, PNG, or WebP). Your image is processed locally in your browser — nothing is uploaded.
  2. Move the cursor over the canvas to preview the pixel color under the eyedropper.
  3. Click anywhere on the image to lock that color as the selected color. Or pick from the web palette below.
  4. Copy the HEX, RGB, or HSL value with a single click. Press Esc to clear the current selection.

What is a Color Picker?

A color picker lets you sample a pixel from any image to extract its color, then produce ready-to-use values in HEX, RGB, and HSL — formats used in CSS, design tools, and image editors.

Use cases

  • Matching brand colors from logos and screenshots.
  • Selecting accent colors for websites and UI design.
  • Building palettes for illustrations and presentations.
  • Writing CSS with ready-to-paste HEX, RGB, or HSL values.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is my image uploaded somewhere?

No. The image is drawn onto a local canvas in your browser and never leaves your device.

What color formats are supported?

HEX (hexadecimal), RGB (red-green-blue), and HSL (hue-saturation-lightness). The companion Color Converter also supports CMYK.

How do I clear the current selection?

Press the Escape key (Esc) anywhere on the page to clear the currently selected color and revert to the default.
