Frequently Asked Questions

What is an amortization schedule?

An amortization schedule shows how each monthly payment is split between principal and interest over the life of the loan. It also shows your remaining balance after each payment.

How is monthly payment calculated?

Monthly payment = P × r(1+r)^n / ((1+r)^n − 1), where P is the loan amount, r is the monthly interest rate (annual rate / 12 / 100), and n is the total number of months.

What is the difference between principal and interest?

The principal is the original loan amount you borrowed. Interest is the cost of borrowing that amount. Early in the loan, more of your payment goes toward interest; later, more goes toward principal.

Can I calculate compound interest with this tool?

This tool calculates loan/mortgage payments with simple interest amortization. For compound interest on investments, use our Compound Interest Calculator.

The loan amortization schedule reveals the hidden truth about long-term loans — that early payments are mostly interest, while later payments go primarily toward the principal. This knowledge helps you make informed decisions about making extra payments to pay off loans faster. Our calculator works for all types of amortized loans including mortgages, car loans, personal loans, and student loans, making it an essential planning tool for anyone considering borrowing money.
